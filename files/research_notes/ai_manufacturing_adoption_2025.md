# AI in Manufacturing 2025

## Real-World Applications

Predictive maintenance leads current implementations, with AI systems analyzing machinery sensor data to forecast failures before they occur, reducing unexpected downtime and costs. Computer vision systems power quality control by scanning products in real-time to identify defects, with multimodal AI combining vision, sound analysis, and sensor data. Digital twins create virtual replicas of processes, production lines, and supply chains to simulate and predict real-time performance. Rolls-Royce leveraged AI-powered digital twins to achieve a 48% increase in time before first engine removal, improving aircraft maintenance efficiency.

AI-powered collaborative robots (cobots) work safely alongside humans, taking on repetitive tasks while employees focus on complex work.
